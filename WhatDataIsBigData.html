What Data is Big Data?

Well it depends!

Majorly on two factors:

  • Volume/Quantity
  • Processing Time
Let's suppose your current compute infrastrucutre can process 100MB of Data in 5 Min.
This much time to process the data is ok with your Business stakeholder.
Hence it is "No Big Data".
Lets's Increase the Data Volume by x 10 = 1000 MB
Time to process: 5Min x 10 = 50 Min.
This is also okay with the business Stakeholder.
Hence it is "No Big Data"
Let's further Increase the Volume by x 10 = 10 GB
Time Time to process: 50Min x 10 = 10 Hours(approx.).
Supposing this is also okay with the business Stakeholder.
Hence it is "No Big Data"
Let's further Increase the Volume by x 10 = 1000 GB(1TB)
Time Time to process: 50 x 10 = 1000 Hours = 40 Days.
40 Days of processing time would be nightmare.
Now, this is a "Big Data Problem" which needs to be solved.
Processing time has to be reduced to a time which suits the Business requirement.
Now this problem can solved by variety of solutions available:
  • Hadoop
  • Spark
  • Flink
  • Etc.
However, Spark is the most popular one today because of the reasons we will discuss in upcoming blogposts.
